Plan B
What is Plan B?
  • Plan B is a morning-after emergency contraception pill.
  • Plan B is not the abortion pill. Plan B prevents a pregnancy, it does not terminate a pregnancy.
  • Plan B works best when you take it within three days after unprotected sex (if taken within five days of unprotected sex, it is 85-90% effective.) Plan B works
  • Plan B works for patients up to 165 pounds in body weight
Where can I get it?

Plan B is availalable for purchase at the Cornell Health Pharmacy

You can also make an appointment at Cornell Health or call Cornell Health nurse with any additional questions!

If your body weight is between 165 lb to 195 lb, you should contact Cornell Health at 607-255-5155 and ask to be connected to Sexual Healthcare for a prescription for Ella. This is available 24/7, and they will send the prescription to whatever pharmacy you want

If your body weight is above 195 lb, you should contact Planned Parenthood at 607-273-1513 and make an appointment for a copper IUD, which is highly-effective contraceptive, for either regular contraception or emergency contraception

Side effects of Plan B

Side effects of Plan B may include...

  • Nausea/Vomiting
  • Dizziness
  • Fatigue
  • Headache
  • Breast Tenderness
  • Lower abdominal pain or cramps
  • Bleeding between periods or heavier menstrual bleeding
  • Potential delay of your period by up to one week (if you don't get your peried within three to four weeks of taking the morning-after pill, take a pregnancy test
Medicines and supplements that affect how well Plan B works

Certain medicines or supplements might affect how well Plan B works including:

  • Certain anti-seizure medicines (such as barbiturates, Felbamate, Topiramate, Oxcarbazepine Carbamazepine, Phenytoin)
  • Rifampin (antibiotic)
  • Griseofulvin (antifungal medication)
  • Certain HIV medicines
  • The herb St. John’s Wort

IUDs
What is an IUD?

An IUD or Intra Uterine Device is a small device inserted into the uterus to prevent pregnancy. It is one of the most common methods of birthcontrol for women today

Where can I get one?

Cornell Health offers special services for IUDs.

Make an appointment.

Ithaca Planned Parenthood also offers IUD services.

Why get one?
  • 99%-plus effectiveness at pregnancy prevention, compared to 91% to 94% effectiveness for oral contraceptives, NuvaRing, the Depo-Provera shot, or birth control patches
  • Hormonal IUD’s only release low levels of birth control hormones locally, so often have fewer and lesser sides effects compared to all other options. All other birth control options release the hormones throughout the body, which increases risk of side effects
  • Temporary and can be removed at any time
  • Many people (not all, but many) stop getting periods with an IUD!
How do they work and what are the options?

All IUD’s are foreign objects in the uterus. IUD's trigger an influx of white blood cells that kill sperm that make it into the uterus and thin the uterine lining which makes it difficult for a fertilized egg to implant

Hormonal IUD’s also release the same hormones as oral contraceptives, but in smaller amounts and locally, resulting in fewer and lesser side effects

  • Kylena: Can stay in for up to 5 years.
  • Mirena: Can stay in for up to 8 years.
  • Liletta: Can stay in for up to 6 years.
  • Skyla: Can stay in for up to 3 years.

Copper IUD’s work because copper is toxic to sperm

  • Paragard: Can stay in for up to 10 years. Also can be inserted as emergency contraceptive
